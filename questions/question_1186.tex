 %Modeling, science, non-linear
 In metereology, the chance of rain over a given area is given by the the following formula
$$P=\overline p a $$
where $P_A$ is the chance of rain, $\overline p$ is the average percentage it will rain over what part of the region may receive rain, and $a$ is the percentage of the region that could receive rain.  In one particular region, a metereologist determines that $$\overline p(a)=0.77a$$ has a very high correlation to the empirical data collected over the last $38$ years.  Which of the following graphs best represents the model of $P_A$?
